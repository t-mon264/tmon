Trillium’s Master the Art of Necessary Cybersecurity Skills


With an absence of a skill roadmap for aspiring cybersecurity professionals, and the non-availability of a standardized assessment criterion, Trillium’s Master the Art of Necessary Cybersecurity Skills (T-MON) has been launched with an aim of developing a benchmark to help guide cybersecurity aspirants and enthusiasts by providing them a clear pathway of the tactics and techniques they need to learn in order to take their career to the next level.
Attack prediction, prevention, detection, and response are the core competencies of a cybersecurity professional. Leveraging our experience in the cybersecurity realm, we aim to help develop these capabilities through interactive and challenge based skill development courses thereby cultivating the next generation of cybersecurity professional.
T-MON is an open-source platform, allowing innovation and value from the collective talent prevailing. We have aligned T-MON with other community led projects such as MITRE ATT&CK Framework and OWASP to provide a robust and pragmatic scheme of studies rooted deeply in the real world exposure of cybersecurity experts to these malicious activities.

Contributions

T-MON is an open source project, and welcomes participation without any requisite affiliation with TISS. Substantive contributions, and feedback, are also invited from all parties, following the common conventions for participation in GitHub public repository projects. TISS team values the principle of continuous improvement, and encourages any efforts towards this goal. Have questions or comments about the content? Used our content and want more? Want to discuss other collaboration opportunities? Please contact us.

Motivation/Mission

With a meteoric rise in the global cyber-footprint, cybersecurity is a rapidly growing field with a rate of growth three times higher than information technology. Yet still, the industry lacks truly skilled professional, in part due to the ever-evolving nature of the domain, and the consequent continued learning.
This is something that can only be offset through combined societal efforts which, by extension, this means that our strategies for the solution must instigate from the heart of our larger corporate social responsibility (CSR) mission. To help give back to the community, we must work together. Hence, we have launched T-MON as an open-source platform, with the objective to collaborate, work together and aid mutual nurturing of cybersecurity professionals of the future, ultimately, reducing the cybersecurity skill shortage.

Approach

With constantly evolving cyber threats, a mentality of preventing all breaches is obsolete. Thus, cybersecurity experts not only need to be prepared to detect and respond to threats, but also need to think like an attacker and develop proactive skills, including, penetration testing, and red teaming tactics to predict and prevent cyber threats. Keeping this in mind, TMON is developed as a multi-perspective (attack-detect-prevent-respond) platform, to instill all the skills vital to protect systems against cyber threats.
T-MON employs a vast array of learning assets from a plethora of sources for each segment, allowing users to customize and navigate through the curriculum as they see fit to aid self-paced learning.
Realistic scenarios in simulated environments featuring the latest attacker tools, tactics and procedures (TTPs)


Learning Objectives


The name T-MON is inspired by the Disney character Timon, a meerkat who took a weak and dejected Simba under his wing and made him realize his true potential of leading the Pride Lands, likewise, we aspire to do the same. Just like a meerkat on the hunt, T-MON is focused on helping the cybersecurity experts become the ultimate predators, rendering the adversaries powerless. Meerkat’s philosophy is to out think, out wit and out play the adversaries and we seek to develop the same philosophy by helping you master these meerkat traits:

Speed and Agility

Pragmatic cybersecurity is all about swiftness; rapid response, and prompt reaction is the only defense, information streams and corporate data assets have. Preemptively detect, and respond to the threats, acting before they become a menace.


Composure

Stay composed against adversity, keeping your focus on the prize at all times.

Resilience

Attack will happen no matter how protected you are. Absolute cybersecurity is a euphoric dream, unachievable due to the rapid pace of development in the technological word. Learn to stay one step ahead, and resilient; like a mongoose, withstanding venomous serpents, and recuperating quickly against them.

Diversity

Diversity is perseverance. Branch out your skillset to be better prepared against modern day cyber-hostilities.

Persistence

Cybersecurity experts need to be persistent to predict, prevent, detect and respond to cybersecurity incidents in an effective and timely manner.



Nondiscriminatory predators

Every attack is equally detrimental to your security. Adversaries don’t distinguish, neither should you.
